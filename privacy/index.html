1. Who we are

easierLet is a property-management service operated by Oakrise Estates Ltd (Company No. 16852456), a UK-registered company. We're the data controller for the personal data described in this policy.

For data-protection queries: privacy@easierlet.com.

2. What data we collect

If you're a landlord using easierLet

  • Your name, email, phone, contact address, company name and number
  • The properties you manage (addresses, photos, certificates)
  • Records you create about your tenants, transactions, maintenance, listings, viewings
  • Subscription and billing details (handled by Stripe — we receive metadata, not card numbers)
  • Audit data: which actions you took, when, and from which IP / device

If you're a tenant or applicant

  • Your name, email, phone, date of birth, nationality
  • Right-to-Rent information (visa type and expiry, if applicable)
  • Employment and income details for affordability and referencing
  • Rental history declarations (previous addresses, previous landlords, arrears, evictions, adverse credit)
  • Maintenance issues you report, replies you send, photos you upload
  • Documents shared with you for your tenancy (agreements, inventories, certificates)
  • Audit data: which actions you took, when, and from which IP / device

If you're a referee (employer, previous landlord, guarantor)

  • Your name, email, phone, address (for guarantors), relationship to the applicant
  • Your responses to a reference form

If you visit our website without signing in

  • Standard server logs (IP, browser, referring page) for security and abuse prevention
  • If you submit a viewing request or apply for a property, the data on that form
  • We don't currently use analytics cookies or tracking pixels. See cookies.

3. Why we use it

  • Run the service. Show your data to you; let landlords and tenants do their work.
  • Tenant referencing. Verify income, employment, rental history.
  • Right-to-Rent compliance. Tenants' nationality and visa info is required by the Immigration Act 2014.
  • Legal record-keeping. Tax records (HMRC), gas safety certificates, deposit-protection records, dispute evidence.
  • Communication. Send maintenance notices, viewing confirmations, agreement signing links, password resets, billing receipts.
  • Security. Detect and prevent fraud or abuse via the audit log.
  • We do not sell personal data. We don't use it for advertising. We don't share it with marketing partners.

5. How long we keep it

The full schedule lives at Data retention periods. Summary:

  • Active tenant data — 6 years after tenancy ends, then anonymised.
  • Rejected applicant data — 6 months, then deleted.
  • Financial records — 7 years (HMRC requirement).
  • Gas safety certificates — 2 years; EICR — 6 years; EPC — 10 years.
  • Viewing requests — 12 months, then anonymised.
  • Audit logs — 7 years.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data (subject to legal retention obligations)
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Object to certain processing
  • Restrict processing while a dispute is resolved
  • Complain to the ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) at ico.org.uk

8. How to make a request

Tenants: open the easierLet portal, tap the profile menu, and use "Download My Data" to generate an instant export, or "Delete My Account" to request erasure.

Landlords and everyone else: email privacy@easierlet.com with your request. We'll verify your identity and respond within 30 days (usually within a week).

9. Cookies

easierLet uses only the cookies necessary to keep you signed in (Supabase auth tokens stored in your browser's localStorage). We don't use analytics, advertising, or tracking cookies. We don't have a cookie consent banner because nothing we set is non-essential.

10. Changes to this policy

We'll update this page when our processing changes. Material changes will be notified by email to active users at least 14 days before they take effect. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page always reflects the current version.
