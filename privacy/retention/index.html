Data retention

How long we keep your data, and why. Last updated 28 April 2026.

UK GDPR requires us to keep personal data only as long as we genuinely need it. Some categories — like tax records — have minimum retention periods set by law. Others — like rejected applications — are kept for a deliberately short window, then anonymised.

Anonymising means we clear the personal identifiers (name, email, phone, DoB, financial details) but keep structural records (tenancy dates, rent, deposit) for tax and audit purposes. Once anonymised, the record can no longer be tied to you.

Retention periods

DataPeriodWhy
Active tenant records 6 years after tenancy ends HMRC tax-record retention + 6-year limitation period for civil claims (Limitation Act 1980). After this, personal identifiers are anonymised; tenancy dates and amounts are kept for tax compliance.
Rejected applicants 6 months Allows you to re-apply if circumstances change. After 6 months we have no ongoing legitimate interest, so the record is fully deleted.
Tenant references Same as tenant record Kept alongside the tenant they relate to. Anonymised at the same time.
Financial records (rent, expenses) 7 years HMRC requires 6 years from the end of the accounting period; we keep 7 to cover edge cases.
Gas Safety certificates (CP12) 2 years The current certificate plus the previous one — covers any gap in successor inspections.
EICR (Electrical Installation Condition Report) 6 years Five-year inspection cycle plus one year after replacement.
EPC (Energy Performance Certificate) 10 years An EPC is statutorily valid for 10 years. We keep it for that period.
Viewing requests 12 months After a year there's no operational need for prospect contact details. Names, emails, phone numbers and messages are anonymised; the booking record itself stays for analytics.
Audit logs 7 years Regulatory compliance and dispute evidence.
Maintenance + tenancy + inventory records Tied to the tenant record If the underlying tenant data is anonymised, the personal references in these records are too.
30-day notice. Before we anonymise active tenant data, we email the landlord 30 days in advance. They can defer with a documented reason (e.g. unresolved dispute, ongoing claim) — otherwise the anonymisation happens automatically on the deadline.

Earlier deletion on request

You can ask us to anonymise or delete your data sooner — we'll do it unless we have a legal obligation to keep it (e.g. tax records). Use the "Download My Data" / "Delete My Account" controls in the tenant portal, or email privacy@easierlet.com.

Backups

Our database provider (Supabase) keeps automated backups for 7 days. Anonymisation propagates to those backups within that window. We don't take additional offsite backups of personal data.
